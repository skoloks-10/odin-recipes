Fried Chicken

Home

I experimented with lots of recipes over the years to find the best crispy fried chicken. This is it! It's bits and pieces of hints I have heard over the years. It's more a matter of preparation and cooking method!

Ingredients

  • 1 (3 to 4 pounds) whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Directions

  1. Place chicken pieces in a large bowl and cover with buttermilk. Cover bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  2. In a large resealable plastic bag, combine flour, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper. Shake to mix well.
  3. Remove chicken pieces from buttermilk, allowing excess to drip off. Place chicken pieces in the bag with the flour mixture, a few pieces at a time; seal bag and shake to coat. Place coated chicken pieces on a wire rack set over a baking sheet; let rest for 10 minutes.
  4. Pour enough oil into a deep heavy skillet or Dutch oven to come halfway up sides of pan; heat oil to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  5. Fry chicken pieces in hot oil until golden brown and juices run clear, about 15 to 18 minutes, turning occasionally. Drain on paper towels before serving.
